Portfolio

My internship this past summer was at Gentex Corporation. This company is most known for their full-display mirrors (FDM) but they are actively involved in other areas of technology as well. Starting on the green team, I was involved in soldering and partially cross-sections, to gain practical skills that can assist me during my time there. Later on, I transitioned into bench-testing to gain skills in electronic testing and troubleshooting. At first, I tested FDM's, finding any issues that may occur by simulating it in a working environment. If a problem occurred, I would go back and look at documentation of the mirrors as well as the schematics to try and find the cause of it. Because I was initially given older models, majority of the time it was due to old electronics, and it was as simple as swapping out and soldering in newer board components. Later on, I was involved in a project that involved testing a Switch-Mode Power Supply (SMPS) board with multiple SMPS units to find a suitable replacement for future designs. (Note that the board seen in the picture is not the actual board, it is an evaluation board from TI. Gentex used this board initially to give us a better understanding of SMPS's before we were brought onto the actual project. I can't show the actual board we used in testing, but I would be happy to discuss about it and how we tested!) This experience gave me insights into the practical challenges of selecting components for electronic designs, and also gave me an appreciation for the immense amount of testing that goes into each product to ensure functionality. After the team finished initial rounds of testing; we were then brought in for transient analysis of the SMPS board. Similarly to before, my role was to find and document any issues found.
Near the end of my internship, I was brought into troubleshoot a newer more complex FDM towards the end of my internship. Because this model was rather new, I was led by a full-time electrical engineer that works there. This allowed me to gain a deeper understanding of the product and its components. Overall, I believe my first internship at Gentex provided me with a good foundation in electronics, testing, and troubleshooting, all of which are valuable in this field.

A future project I want to do is turning a Wii into a portable hand-held device. I plan on making two iterations of this, once by trimming the Wii board itself and designing my own 3D printed board, and secondly to create a handheld device with a Raspberry Pi. For the first iteration, I think by trimming the board and implementing some electronics, it will provide a good experience working with circuit boards, getting more familiar with the elements on the board. While the Raspberry Pi won't be able to run the games as well, it will provide a better learning experience when it comes to programming. The video to the right inspired me to try something like this out myself. If I succeed in doing this, I may even try to branch out and make other emulated hand-held devices.
