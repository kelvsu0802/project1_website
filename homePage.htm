About Me:

Howdy! My name is Kelvin Su, a junior in Computer Engineering at Texas A&M University-College Station. My journey in the realm of computer engineering began with admiring my parents. My dad was an electrical engineer, and my mom was in Computer Science, and as a result I've always been intrigued by the intersection of hardware and software. I always aim to gain some understanding of the intricacies that make our digital world work seamlessly. Being a student at Texas A&M, I've been able to engage in a curriculum that hones my technical skills, but also fosters a collaborative mindset. From simply learning the basics to designing circuits, I've found every challenge a fun opportunity to learn and grow. I try to stay updated on the latest advancements in the computer engineering to stay updated in the ever-changing field of computer engineering. Predictably, my hobbies involves dabbling in electronics. Whether it be experimenting with microcontrollers, soldering components together, or simply learning a new skill in the realm of electronics, I find the joy in the hands-on exploration. It also doubles as a good learning experience for my classes in university. More recently, I've been interested in teaching myself C and utilizing that with an Arduino, building small-scale fun projects. I take a online course unrelated to my university, and I've found it quite fun. It helps that because my university teaches C++ at a high level, the transition to C hasn't been too difficult. My hopes in this is that I will develop a stronger understanding of microcontrollers, and can move onto more professional/advanced microcontrollers so that I can create bigger projects.

Outside of school, I enjoy playing ping-pong, going on walks, trying out new restaurants (especially small family ran ones!), and am an avid gamer. In fact, I specifically built my keyboard, and plan on learning a little bit of woodworking so I can make myself a desk, all to create for myself my ideal setup to game on.
